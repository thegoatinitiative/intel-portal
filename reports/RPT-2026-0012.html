Tactical Infiltration and Evidence Evasion

The recovery of two identity documents for the same individual under completely different biographical legends represents a textbook dual-identity operational security (OPSEC) configuration. The Iranian diplomatic passport (D10005432) establishes the subject's true identity as Issa Rezaei, a senior MODAFL official with the rank of Deputy Minister of Defense and Armed Forces Logistics. The Venezuelan cédula (V-24.041.282) under the name "Simon Thomas Yokhana" represents a fully backstopped cover identity issued through Venezuela's compromised SAIME apparatus.

The cédula was issued on 11 June 2015, when Dante Rivas was serving as Director of SAIME — his second tenure in the role after being re-appointed by President Nicolás Maduro on 27 May 2015. This timing is significant: Rivas had previously been removed from SAIME in 2012 by Hugo Chávez, and his reinstatement coincided with the acceleration of Iran-Venezuela defense cooperation. The document bears Rivas's printed name and the code MM755.

The alias construction is deliberate and sophisticated. "Simon Thomas Yokhana" presents as an Assyrian Christian name — a minority community with diaspora populations across the Middle East and Latin America. This ethnoreligious masking serves two purposes: it provides a plausible explanation for Middle Eastern physiological features while completely obscuring any connection to the Iranian government or Shia establishment. The 3-year date-of-birth discrepancy (1963 vs. 1966) is within the margin that complicates but does not prevent biometric age estimation, suggesting awareness of facial recognition age-verification tolerances.
