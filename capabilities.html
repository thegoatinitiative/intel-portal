The Intelligence Gap

The Problem: No Collection Without Presence

Government contractors deliver finished intelligence products to agencies and decision-makers. But the quality of any finished product is only as good as the collection that feeds it. In the San Diego sector — the most active and geographically punishing smuggling corridor on the U.S.-Mexico border — that collection requires something no amount of OSINT or remote analysis can replace: persistent physical presence on the ground.

Sanctuary State Barrier

California law prohibits state and local agencies from cooperating directly with federal authorities on immigration matters. This creates intelligence silos that traditional collection approaches cannot penetrate. TGI already serves as the trusted neutral conduit between all levels of law enforcement — a role that took years to build and cannot be replicated remotely.

The HUMINT Gap

Building a HUMINT network on both sides of the U.S.-Mexico border from scratch takes years and significant personal risk. No contractor can stand this up on a contract timeline. TGI's community network, modeled after Green Beret partnership strategies, is already operational and producing.

The SIA Blind Spot

Special Interest Aliens from China, the Middle East, and Africa are using cartel infrastructure to enter undetected. Identifying them requires pattern-of-life intelligence from persistent local presence — not remote analysis. TGI has already documented multiple SIA networks and shared intelligence with federal partners.
